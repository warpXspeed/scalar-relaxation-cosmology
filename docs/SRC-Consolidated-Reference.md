Core Philosophy
The universe is built on one single substrate: the Energy Crystal (φ) — a structured potential-energy lattice with intrinsic geometry, frequency-dependent response, nonlinear stress behavior, and self-organizing capabilities.
There is no empty space. Space is filled with this dynamic medium. All observed phenomena — gravity, light, redshift, galactic structure, stellar cycles, cosmic-scale patterns — emerge from the same rules operating at different scales. No ad-hoc particles or forces are added.
Key Mechanisms

Galactic Currents: Large-scale coherent energy flows through the φ lattice. They are the primary organizers.
Locally: Feed and stabilize stars (e.g., the Sun as a driven toroidal resonator).
Globally: Form the cosmic web connecting galaxies and clusters.
They create persistent density gradients and longitudinal compression ("pinch") that maintains flat rotation curves without dark matter.

Variable Lattice Density: The φ medium is not homogeneous. Regions with higher ordered structure and stronger currents are denser/tighter → slower local expansion/relaxation.
Redshift as Propagation Effect:
Light travels through regions of varying lattice density and galactic current strength.
Higher density zones cause stronger cumulative wavelength stretching and resonant dissipation.
The observed Hubble-like redshift-distance relation is an average over many different paths through this inhomogeneous medium.
Variations in aether density naturally explain scatter and anomalies in redshift data.


Why This Fits JWST Better
JWST has revealed surprisingly mature, massive galaxies at high reported redshifts. In standard cosmology these are extremely difficult to explain (not enough time).
In SRC:

A significant portion of observed redshift comes from propagation effects through varying φ density rather than pure cosmic distance/time.
Many high-z galaxies are therefore closer in cosmic time than standard models assume, giving them more time to form and evolve.
No need for extreme early star-formation efficiency or massive dark matter halos. The same galactic currents that organize mature galaxies locally also operate at high redshift.

This single-substrate view removes many of the "impossible early galaxy" tensions without adding new physics.
Single-Substrate Consistency

Local (Solar System / Galaxy): Galactic currents feed stellar resonators → stable magnetic cycles, layered rotation, flat rotation curves.
Cosmological: Light propagates through the same current-filled, density-varying medium → average redshift relation + natural variations.
No rule changes between scales. One medium, one set of mechanisms.

Advantages Over Standard Physics

Space is not empty → no need to invent dark matter particles or dark energy as separate entities.
Redshift is a real propagation effect through a structured medium.
Galactic currents provide continuous organizing energy at all scales.
Better alignment with JWST observations of early mature structure.
Gravity, EM, and other forces emerge as different stress/strain modalities of the same lattice.
