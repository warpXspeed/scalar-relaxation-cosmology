The Overstated Role of CO₂Mainstream science claims human-emitted CO₂ (now 0.04% of the atmosphere) traps heat via the greenhouse effect, driving ~1–1.1°C warming since pre-industrial times and most extreme weather. This rests on radiative forcing (1–2 W/m² from CO₂) amplified by feedbacks like water vapor.But empirical data contradicts this as the dominant cause:CO₂'s infrared absorption is largely saturated in lower atmospheric bands; additional molecules add marginal effect (logarithmic, not linear). Lab spectroscopy and satellite measurements show diminishing returns—extra CO₂ mostly affects upper altitudes with little surface impact.
Water vapor dominates the greenhouse effect (~66–85%), yet it's temperature-controlled (feedback), not driver. CO₂ is claimed to initiate via forcing, but natural variability (solar, oceanic cycles) better explains pre-1950 warming without significant emissions.
