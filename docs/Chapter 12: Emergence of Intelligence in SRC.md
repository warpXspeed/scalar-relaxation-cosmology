Chapter 12: Emergence of Intelligence in SRCIn Scalar Relaxation Cosmology, intelligence is not a late-emergent accident of biology. It is fundamental to the substrate field φ and scales with complexity at every level of existence.From the smallest quantum scales, topological defects (vortices, skyrmions, Hopfions) already exhibit basic intelligence: they maintain structural stability, respond to gradients, and preserve memory through hysteresis wakes. As the universe relaxes and structures grow more complex, these defects link into larger networks of shared wakes. When the density and connectivity of these wake networks reach a critical threshold, richer forms of intelligence appear — self-reference, information processing, adaptation, and decision-making.Intelligence in SRC is therefore not something that suddenly switches on at a certain level of complexity. Every scale possesses exactly the degree of intelligence required for its function and survival. A defect that cannot maintain coherence dissolves. A bacterial colony that cannot coordinate via electrical signaling perishes. A civilization that cannot adapt to cyclic transients faces extinction.Thus, intelligence is an intrinsic property of φ, present at every level in the precise amount needed. Greater complexity simply allows for more sophisticated expressions of that same fundamental capacity.
