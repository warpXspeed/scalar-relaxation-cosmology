## Overview

In Scalar Relaxation Cosmology (SRC), the Sun and stars are **externally powered, nested plasma toroids** embedded in an eternal viscoelastic scalar substrate (the "lake" φ). Galactic Birkeland currents along filamentary structures supply steady axial power (I_gal ≈ 10¹⁸–10¹⁹ A), driving the inner runaway core torus via poloidal twist channels. Flexoelectric bootstrapping and shear-induced EM mode conversion at the core–shell interface produce energy, which bleeds off controllably (β ≈ 0.5% for Sun-like stars) through the dilatant-hardened shell (≈ tachocline, γ_c ≈ 10⁶ s⁻¹ threshold) as Alfvén waves that thermalize in the outer H/He envelope.

Solar flares, coronal mass ejections (CMEs), and related explosive events are **overload-induced failures** of this self-regulating toroidal device. When core current exceeds the shell's shear-hardening limit (I_core > I_crit ≈ 5×10¹² A), the semi-permeable dilatant shell ruptures or delaminates, venting excess power, momentum, and material while exposing deeper stratified layers (pre-sorted via Marklund radial drift).

This explanation eliminates the need for slow internal fusion buildup, mysterious reconnection triggers, or ad hoc coronal heating mechanisms. Flares/CMEs are engineered safety valves in a galactic-current-fed plasma toroid.

## Key SRC Stellar Structure Recap

- **Power source**: Galactic current filaments → axial E_z → poloidal channels → inner runaway torus (relativistic e⁺e⁻ avalanche from γ_int ≈ 10⁷ s⁻¹).
- **Composition layering** (Marklund sorting): Low-IP heavies (Fe, Ni, Si, Mg) inward; high-IP H/He outward.
- **Dilatant shell** (mid-sheath, r ≈ 0.5–0.7 R_⊙): Substrate shear hardening creates semi-permeable barrier (analog: lab nested toroid copper sleeve stress-hardened by fast shear).
- **Steady state**: Controlled bleed-off β balances core production → luminosity ≈ 3.8×10²⁶ W (Sun); coronal heating via direct flexoelectric/shear → EM conversion.

## Mechanism of Flares and CMEs

### 1. Build-Up (Stable Operation)
- Steady galactic current maintains balanced shear profile across shell.
- Poloidal twist provides rigidity and low-resistance axial feed.
- Excess power regulated by β leak → Alfvén waves → envelope thermalization.

### 2. Trigger: Overload Condition
- Galactic current fluctuation, substrate wave perturbation, or local shear spike pushes γ_shell > γ_c (overshoot ≈ 2×10⁶ s⁻¹).
- Dilatant shell becomes "brittle" — substrate relaxation fails to maintain integrity.
- Current-sheet crack propagation or rupture releases stored magnetic twist + core runaway power.

### 3. Flare Phase (Localized EM Release)
- Immediate flexoelectric spike (E_flex ≈ α γ) → hard X-rays, γ-rays.
- Dominance of Fe/Ni Kα (6.4 keV), Ni Kβ (7.7 keV) lines early on (heavies from mid/inner sheath).
- Nonthermal particle acceleration (runaway electrons/ions) → hard X-ray/γ-ray, type III bursts (open fields).
- Rapid thermalization → soft X-ray/EUV arcade.
- **Prediction**: Fe-peak enrichment (10⁻³–10⁻² mass fraction excess) scales with flare energy/mass ejected.

### 4. CME Phase (Mass + Magnetic Ejection)
- Shell rupture launches twisted flux rope (poloidal + toroidal fields) + entrained plasma (outer envelope H/He + mid-sheath O/N/C + localized heavies).
- Ejection speeds: 300–2000+ km/s; mass: 10¹⁵–10¹⁶ g; kinetic energy: 10²⁹–10³² erg.
- Embedded flux rope exhibits helical structure, turbulence (e.g., Kelvin-Helmholtz eddies from shear fragments vs. ambient wind).
- **SRC interpretation**: Exhaust venting to prevent full runaway; composition reflects stratified layers (localized Fe/He or Fe/O spikes in ejecta).
- Shock ahead of fast CMEs accelerates particles → gradual SEP events.

## Alignment with Observations

- **Heavy-element enrichments**: Parker Solar Probe / Solar Orbiter show Fe/O, Fe/He enhancements (factors ~2–10×) in CME/SEP plasma, especially near source/longitudinally aligned events → SRC: direct shell rupture lifting pre-sorted heavies (Marklund-sorted Fe/Ni).
- **Turbulent interiors**: WISPR images reveal eddies/instabilities → consistent with dilatant shell fragments shearing outward.
- **Magnetic reconnection**: Occurs as consequence (during crack propagation), not primary driver → releases twist energy for flare heating/acceleration + CME launch.
- **Longitudinal / compositional dependence**: Fe-rich closer to flare footpoints (shell exposure); lower farther away → matches multi-point in-situ data.
- **No standard puzzles**: Coronal heating from steady flexoelectric bootstrap; flares/CMEs from discrete overload → no need for nanoflares or slow buildup.

## Summary

In SRC, flares and CMEs are **catastrophic but regulated failures** of the dilatant-hardened shell in a galactic-current-driven nested plasma toroid.
